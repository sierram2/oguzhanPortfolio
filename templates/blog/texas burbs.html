texas burbs

April 9, 2026

I'd like to live somewhere I don't need to drive. A walkable city with clean air and local bakeries. Somewhere that doesn't get too cold, and the people don't try to kill me. The life I have is very good, but I don't want to get old here. The food is all so highly processed, the people are too conservative and close-minded, and it feels like everything is trying to take your money. Not even just my money, but my attention, my sleep, my space, my data…. And then I'm taxed when I earn, taxed when I spend, taxed when my earnings have earnings, taxed where I live, 8.25% of what I eat, while I sleep.

Property taxes in Texas are so insane. I see probably 283 advertisements while just going to the grocery store down the street. A car wash on every corner, but no sidewalks anywhere. Strip malls line Bandera, hoping to pull you off your soul-sucking commute home to suburban hell. Who is planning the Culebra area? They really make me believe I am never less qualified for a job than I think. Thousands of people funneled down a two-lane road for miles. 7am traffic finds new lows.

I'd never even considered improving my life until recently. I've always been content. I've never thought about eating fresh local fruit from a farmer's market near the sea in the afternoon. I've never considered really retiring early and taking more time to rest. Capitalism in America makes one hyper-individualist. I earn to exist, and I exist to earn. I thought it was normal to eat in the car. I've always had a slight obsession with productivity. Even though I'm not sure if I will ever have kids, I'm considering the costs of housing and how that will shape the quality of their schools and consequently, their life. But what if I didn't do any of that.

What if I just worked for a few hours a day, watched the sun crawl across the sky, and never sat in 1604 traffic again? I want to eat eggs from a chicken who saw daylight, is that so crazy? and not have everything wrapped in 4 layers of plastic. I'd like to buy my clothes from someone who sews and makes pieces for life. I want to paint and not think of it as a waste of money and space and time. I want to hear the birds sing sweetly as I drink tea next to the people I love as I grow old.
